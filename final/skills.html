Background

I have often been the "front-line" defense. I was always the person who received a request first and would handle it or pass it along and ask for help. I've worked as a tutor/teacher's assistant for some time now, going on 3 years. I have been entrusted in taking a rubric, grading students and helping those who needed help without doing the work for them. The greatest non-technical thing I've learned from this is how important it is to avoid jargon or to change your vocabulary on the fly to allow the non-technical person to understand what you're trying to help them with. It's about being precise and using metaphors that they can understand if trying to explain something inherently difficult.
